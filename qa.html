Frequently Asked Questions

During an earthquake:

  • Drop to the ground
  • Take cover under a sturdy desk or table
  • Hold on until the shaking stops
  • Stay away from windows and exterior walls
  • If you're in bed, stay there and protect your head with a pillow

Essential items for an emergency kit:

  • Water (one gallon per person per day for at least three days)
  • Non-perishable food (at least a three-day supply)
  • Battery-powered radio and extra batteries
  • Flashlight and extra batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Whistle to signal for help
  • Dust masks, plastic sheeting, and duct tape
  • Moist towelettes, garbage bags, and plastic ties
  • Manual can opener
  • Cell phone with chargers and backup battery

To stay informed during a disaster:

  • Keep your mobile phone charged and have backup power banks ready
  • Follow local emergency services on social media
  • Have a battery-powered or hand-crank radio for emergency broadcasts
  • Sign up for local emergency alerts
  • Keep in touch with family and neighbors
  • Check official government websites for updates
